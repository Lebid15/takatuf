I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Medical Technology Engineering

   As a result of the substantial scientific and technological development that affected many disciplines, many branches have appeared within the specialisation of engineering, including medical technology engineering, which is also called biomedical engineering. This field is concerned with studying the body from an engineering point of view, in order to design suitable artificial limbs, organs, or other medical devices.

   Medical technology engineering helps doctors define their tasks and facilitates their work, so the medical device has become an essential assistant for doctors in all steps, starting from diagnosis to the end of the treatment course. The need for development in this field has made designing medical devices a great priority, and this led to the involvement of electrical, mechanical and computer engineers, whose knowledge must include all aspects of the human body — from biology, anatomy and physiology.

   Medical devices can be divided into two parts: diagnostic medical devices, most notably the ultrasound device, and medical therapeutic devices used in treatments. Another classification includes three categories: simple devices such as bandages and thermometers; more complex devices that require caution, such as those that emit X-rays; and devices requiring deep study before use, such as the valves used in the artificial heart or artificial joints.

   Medical engineers work in many fields, including well-equipped hospitals, medical companies, and advanced research centres in universities. They work in cooperation with all disciplines, including nursing, medicine, biology, and others.

   In short, biomedical engineering has improved healthcare to a great extent. Furthermore, new technology — especially 3D printing — has improved the design process. Biomedical engineers can develop new procedures using knowledge from many technical sources and conduct research needed to solve clinical problems.

II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Throughout history, there have always been people who designed and built tools to solve problems or improve lives. The concept of engineering has been around since ancient times, when primitive engineers created basic inventions such as the pulley, the lever, and the wheel. Each of these inventions makes use of basic mechanical principles.

   Some of the earliest and most impressive examples of engineering date back to ancient Egypt. Imhotep, who lived around 2650 BC, is considered the first engineer in recorded history. He designed and executed the construction of the Pyramid of Djoser and is also credited with first using columns in architecture.

   Arab engineers made outstanding contributions during the Islamic Golden Age. They designed the spinning wheel in the early eleventh century, which was essential to the growth of the cotton industry. They also developed wind-powered machines such as the windmill and the wind pump. These innovations spread across the world and influenced engineering for centuries.

   The Industrial Revolution in the 18th and 19th centuries brought dramatic changes to engineering. Steam engines, railways, and mass production transformed societies around the world. Today, engineering has expanded into countless specialised fields, including civil, mechanical, electrical, aerospace, and biomedical engineering, each playing a vital role in modern life.

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

"Write about the role of engineering in improving people's lives."
Engineering has played an essential role in transforming human life throughout history. From the invention of the wheel in ancient times to the development of modern biomedical devices, engineers have constantly solved problems and made life easier, safer, and more comfortable.

In the field of medicine, biomedical engineering has produced remarkable inventions such as artificial limbs, pacemakers, and MRI machines. These devices have saved countless lives and given many people the chance to live normally. Civil engineering has built bridges, roads, and buildings that connect communities, while electrical and computer engineering have powered the digital revolution that has changed the way we communicate and work.

In conclusion, engineering is one of the most important fields in modern society. Without it, the world would be very different. We owe much of our comfort and quality of life to the creativity and hard work of engineers around the world.
