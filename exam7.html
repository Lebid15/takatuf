I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Medical Inventions

   Over the years, technology has played a significant role in developing medical science. Advances in medicine have created alternatives to dangerous or even ineffective procedures. Breakthroughs have found new solutions to historical medical challenges and have opened up possibilities beyond what doctors thought was impossible years ago. Today's techniques, surgeries and drugs have decreased the overall deaths among humans. Medical inventions like X-ray Imaging, Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Robotic Surgery and Organ Transplants have changed medicine forever.

   An X-ray is a common imaging test that has been used for decades. It is an image created on a photographic film or electronically on a digital system, using external radiation to produce images of the body. X-ray Imaging can help doctors view the inside of the patient's body without having to make an incision. This can help surgeons diagnose, monitor and treat many medical conditions.

   Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a medical imaging technique used in radiology to form pictures of the anatomy and physiological processes of the body. MRI scanners use strong magnetic fields and radio waves to generate images of the organs in the body. MRI allows access to soft tissues and bone marrow in cases of inflammation and infection.

   Robotic Surgery is usually associated with minimally invasive procedures. During Robotic Surgery, surgeons can perform very complex steps with more precision, flexibility and control than is possible with conventional techniques. Compared to traditional open surgery, Robotic Surgery results in smaller incisions, which reduce pain and lead to less time in hospital and quicker recovery.

   In summary, the emerging medical devices and technologies are changing the face of 21st century medical practice. People born with diseases or body defects can now be healed with the help of advanced technology. Various advances in the medical field have saved millions of lives and improved many others.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)

Q2. How does X-ray Imaging help doctors without making an incision? (06 pts)

Q3. Why do surgeons prefer Robotic Surgery to conventional surgery? (06 pts)
Because it allows them to perform complex steps with more precision, flexibility and control, resulting in smaller incisions, less pain, and quicker recovery.

II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Throughout history, medicine has evolved from basic herbal remedies to highly sophisticated scientific procedures. In ancient times, people relied on plants, herbs and spiritual rituals to treat illness. The practice of medicine in the Middle Ages was mainly based on experience and focused on curing illnesses rather than understanding their causes. Treatments were often a mixture of religion, herbal remedies and procedures that we would now consider harmful.

   A major turning point came during the Islamic Golden Age, when scholars such as Ibn Sina (Avicenna) and Al-Razi made outstanding contributions to medicine. Ibn Sina's encyclopaedia "The Canon of Medicine" remained a standard medical text in Europe for centuries. Al-Razi was the first to distinguish between smallpox and measles and to use animal gut for surgical stitches.

   Modern medicine began to emerge in the late 18th century, following the Industrial Revolution. In the 1800s, physicians discovered that illnesses were caused by germs. This led to a new focus on hygiene and cleanliness in hospitals. The 20th century brought further breakthroughs including antibiotics, vaccines, and new surgical techniques that have saved countless lives.

   Today, medical technology continues to advance at an extraordinary rate. Artificial intelligence is now being used to diagnose diseases, while robotic systems assist surgeons in performing precise operations. The future of medicine looks bright, with the promise of personalised treatments based on individual genetic profiles.

Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

"Write about a recent medical invention that has had a great impact on healthcare."
One of the most remarkable medical inventions of recent times is Robotic Surgery. This technology allows surgeons to perform complex operations with extreme precision, flexibility, and control — all through tiny incisions in the patient's body.

Unlike traditional open surgery, robotic procedures result in smaller cuts, less blood loss, reduced pain, and a much faster recovery time. Patients can often return home within days rather than weeks. This invention has been particularly useful in delicate operations such as heart surgery, cancer treatment, and joint replacement.

In my opinion, Robotic Surgery represents the future of medicine. Although it is still expensive and not available in every hospital, its benefits are undeniable. As technology continues to advance, this invention will become more accessible and will save even more lives around the world.
