I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Syrian Nationality and Citizenship

   Nationality Law is the law governing the acquisition, transmission and loss of Syrian citizenship. Syrian citizenship is the status of being a citizen of the Syrian Arab Republic and it can be obtained by birth or naturalisation. The Syrian Nationality Law was enacted in 1969.

   The Syrian nationality is determined predominantly by paternity — that is, by the father. The place of birth is irrelevant, and being born in Syria does not grant an automatic right to Syrian nationality. Birth to a Syrian mother does not automatically confer nationality. If a Syrian woman marries a foreign husband, their children will have the foreign husband's nationality and have no claim to Syrian nationality, even if they were born and raised in Syria.

   The other way to acquire Syrian nationality is through naturalisation. Non-nationals who have resided in the country for over 5 years and fulfilled a number of other requirements can apply for naturalisation. The requirements are eased for individuals from another Arab country. A foreign woman who marries a Syrian man can naturalise on the basis of that marriage, but a foreign husband cannot acquire Syrian citizenship on the basis of marriage to a Syrian woman.

   Citizenship is not only the state of being vested with the rights and duties of a citizen, but it can also be defined as the character of an individual who is viewed as a member of a particular society. Although Syrian citizenship provides many rights, it also involves many responsibilities. Every Syrian citizen must obey state laws, pay taxes, and contribute positively to society.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. How can Syrian citizenship be obtained according to the text? (06 pts)
Syrian citizenship can be obtained by birth or through naturalisation.
Q2. Why might children born in Syria to a Syrian mother not have Syrian nationality? (06 pts)
Because Syrian nationality is determined by the father (paternity). If the father is foreign, the children take the father's nationality.
Q3. What conditions must a non-national meet to apply for naturalisation? (06 pts)
They must have resided in Syria for over 5 years and fulfilled other requirements. Requirements are eased for individuals from Arab countries.

   Citizenship is not simply a legal status; it is a way of life. A good citizen is someone who actively participates in the life of their community, respects the rights of others, and fulfils their responsibilities towards society. The concept of good citizenship includes a wide range of behaviours and attitudes.

   Good citizens respect the law. They understand that laws exist to protect everyone and to maintain order in society. They pay their taxes, vote in elections, and participate in community activities. They are honest in their dealings with others and do not engage in corruption or dishonest behaviour.

   Good citizens are also caring and helpful. They assist neighbours in need, volunteer for community projects, and support charitable causes. They show respect for public property and the environment by not littering or causing damage. In times of crisis, they work together with others to find solutions.

   Finally, good citizens are aware of their rights. They know that they have the right to express their opinions freely, to receive fair treatment under the law, and to participate in the democratic process. At the same time, they recognise that rights come with responsibilities, and they are committed to using their rights in a responsible and constructive way.

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

"Write about what it means to be a good citizen in your community."
Being a good citizen means much more than simply having a passport or an identity card. It means actively participating in the life of your community and fulfilling your responsibilities towards others.

A good citizen respects the law and pays taxes honestly, knowing that these contributions support public services such as schools, hospitals, and roads. They also participate in elections to choose representatives who will act in the best interests of the community. Beyond legal duties, a good citizen is caring and helpful — they assist neighbours, volunteer for community projects, and protect public property.

In my opinion, the most important quality of a good citizen is awareness. Knowing your rights is important, but knowing your responsibilities is even more so. If every individual in society committed to being a good citizen, our communities would be safer, fairer, and more prosperous for everyone.
