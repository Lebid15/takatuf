I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Culture shock is an experience people may have when they move to a foreign country with a new cultural environment, which is different from their own. These unfamiliar surroundings can lead to a feeling of being a little bit lost.

   People experiencing culture shock usually move through four stages; they will initially have the honeymoon stage which is characterised by a positive feeling that everything is new and exciting. Then there will be the frustration stage, it may be marked by rejection of the new culture, as well as romanticising one's home culture. But then, with some time and perhaps help from locals, people will start the adjustment stage and begin to feel more familiar and comfortable with the new environment. Adaption and acceptance is the final stage which contributes to the successful integration.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. What is culture shock? (06 pts)
Culture shock is an experience people may have when they move to a foreign country with a new cultural environment which is different from their own, leading to a feeling of being a little lost.
Q2. What are the four stages of culture shock? (06 pts)
The four stages are: 1) the honeymoon stage, 2) the frustration stage, 3) the adjustment stage, and 4) the adaption and acceptance stage.
Q3. How do people start to feel better? (06 pts)
With some time and perhaps help from locals, people start to feel more familiar and comfortable with the new environment during the adjustment stage.
Find words in the text which mean the following: (12 marks)
Q4. An unhappy and worried mental state. (06 pts)
frustration
Q5. The process of fitting into a community. (06 pts)
integration
Rewrite these sentences about the text to correct the information: (12 marks)
Q6. Refusing one's culture is one of the characteristics of honeymoon stage. (06 pts)
Refusing one's culture is one of the characteristics of the frustration stage, not the honeymoon stage.
Q7. You will start to feel comfortable in the second stage. (06 pts)
You will start to feel comfortable in the third stage (the adjustment stage), not the second.
II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Human rights are the rights which one acquires by being alive, while civil rights are the rights that one obtains by being a legal member of a certain political life. In other words, civil rights are the rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality. They guarantee equal social opportunities and equal protection under the law, regardless of race, religion, or other personal characteristics.

   Civil and political rights are a class of rights that protect individuals' freedom from violation by governments, social organizations, and private individuals. They ensure one's right to participate in the civil and political life of society and the state without discrimination or oppression. Unlike other rights concepts, such as human rights or natural rights, in which people acquire rights inherently, perhaps from God or nature, civil rights must be given and guaranteed by the power of the State.

   Examples of civil rights include the right to vote, the right to a fair trial, the right to government services, the right to a public education, and the right to use public facilities. Civil rights are an essential component of democracy; when individuals are prevented from participating in political society, their civil rights are being denied.

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

Engineering has played a vital part in people's lives today. It has become a must for improving our life. Write an article about the role of engineering in the modern world.
The following prompts may help you:
  • advantages of engineering
  • public health and safety
  • technology and quality of life
Engineering is one of the most important fields in the modern world. It has transformed the way we live, work, and communicate. Without engineering, we would not have hospitals, bridges, computers, or clean water systems.

One of the greatest advantages of engineering is its contribution to public health and safety. Engineers design medical equipment, water treatment facilities, and safe buildings that protect people's lives every day.

Moreover, engineering drives technology and improves quality of life. From smartphones to electric cars, engineering innovations make our daily lives more comfortable and efficient. In conclusion, engineering is the backbone of modern civilisation and its role will only grow in the future.
