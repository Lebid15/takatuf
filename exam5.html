I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the branch of science and engineering specialised in making clever machines, capable of thinking and imitating human-level intelligence. One of the primary targets of AI field is to produce fully intelligent agents that interact with the surroundings and conduct human-like behaviours. These agents are tools, having the ability to judge matter and differentiate what is wrong.

   The AI field has started with robots, which can sense and react to the world around them. They also cover purely software-based agents, which can be activated with natural language and multimedia. Currently, the field expands to driving, aviation, medicine, online advertising, image recognition, and personal assistance. The AI field is based on computer science, information engineering, mathematics, psychology, linguistics, philosophy and many other fields. The first spark started in the year 1950, when a test of the ability of a machine to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to that of a human was done.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. What is the main goal of AI? (06 pts)
The main goal of AI is to produce fully intelligent agents that interact with the surroundings and conduct human-like behaviours.
Q2. What are the uses of AI nowadays? (06 pts)
AI currently expands to driving, aviation, medicine, online advertising, image recognition, and personal assistance.
Q3. How did the idea of artificial intelligence come to the human mind? (06 pts)
The first spark of AI started in 1950, when a test of the ability of a machine to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to that of a human was done.

III– Choose the correct answer a, b, c, or d: (54 marks)
Q14. ……… something warm. It's cold outside. (06 pts)
a- pick up    b- put on    c- get out    d- take off
b- put on (to put on clothes = to wear them)
Q15. Each color in the flag has a specific ……… (06 pts)
a- simple    b- symbolize    c- symbolic    d- symbol
d- symbol (noun needed after "a specific")
Q16. One of my friends is going to ……… her hair orange. (06 pts)
a- dye    b- bled    c- drain    d- draw
a- dye (to dye hair = to colour it)
Q17. ……… my mother ……… my father is a teacher. (06 pts)
a- Both / and    b- Neither / nor    c- Neither / or    d- Either / no
a- Both / and (Both my mother and my father are teachers)
Q18. My sister is very polite. She doesn't ……… (06 pts)
a- follow her heart    b- speak out of turn    c- jump out of her skin    d- cost an arm and a leg
b- speak out of turn (idiom = to say something rude or inappropriate)
Q19. She has many friends. She has a ……… face. (06 pts)
a- lose face    b- save face    c- wide face    d- narrow face
c- wide face (a person with many friends is described as having a "wide face" — Arabic expression translated to English: وجه واسع)
Q20. ……… didn't learn to swim because he's afraid ……… water. (06 pts)
a- of    b- in    c- at    d- from
a- of (afraid of water)
Q21. You are in a railway carriage. It's crowded and hot. All the windows are closed. Ask for permission to open one. (06 pts)
a- Yes, of course    b- I'm afraid that's not possible    c- Would you mind if I open the window?    d- No problem
c- Would you mind if I open the window? (polite request for permission)
Q22. The word "so" has the same vowel sound as in ……… (06 pts)

1- Write a composition about the role of robots to enhance human healthcare as you imagine in the future.

2- Many people visit your country, but they don't know a lot about it. Write an article to help them be culturally aware of your country.
Topic 1 – Model Answer:

In the future, robots will play a crucial role in transforming healthcare. I imagine a world where intelligent robots assist doctors in performing delicate surgeries with greater precision and fewer errors. Robotic nurses could monitor patients around the clock, detecting any changes in their condition and alerting medical staff instantly.

Moreover, AI-powered diagnostic robots could analyse medical data and identify diseases at an early stage, significantly improving survival rates. In remote areas where medical professionals are scarce, robots could provide basic healthcare services to those who need them most.

In conclusion, the role of robots in healthcare will be revolutionary, saving countless lives and making medical services more accessible and efficient for everyone.
