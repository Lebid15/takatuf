I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   The Immune System

   The immune system has a vital role since it protects your body from harmful substances, germs and cell changes that could make you ill. As long as it is working properly, you don't notice that it's there. But if it stops working properly — because it's weak or cannot fight particularly aggressive germs — you get ill. Germs that your body has never encountered before are also likely to make you ill. Some germs will only make you ill the first time you come into contact with them.

   The immune system has three main tasks: to fight harmful things that enter our body from the outside or harmful changes that occur inside our body; to eliminate bacteria, viruses, parasites and fungi; and to recognise and neutralise harmful substances from the environment.

   The immune system can be activated by a lot of different things that the body doesn't recognise as its own. These are called antigens. Examples of antigens include the proteins on the surfaces of bacteria and viruses. When these antigens attach to special receptors on the immune cells, a whole series of processes are triggered in the body.

   Once the body has come into contact with a disease-causing germ for the first time, it usually stores information about the germ and how to fight it. Then, if it comes into contact with the germ again, it recognises the germ straight away and can start fighting it more quickly. This is the principle behind vaccination — the vaccine introduces a harmless form of the germ into the body, teaching the immune system to recognise it without causing illness.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. What are the three main tasks of the immune system? (06 pts)
1) To fight harmful things from outside or changes inside the body. 2) To eliminate bacteria, viruses, parasites and fungi. 3) To recognise and neutralise harmful substances from the environment.
Q2. What are antigens and why are they important? (06 pts)
Antigens are things the body does not recognise as its own (e.g. proteins on bacteria). They activate the immune system by triggering a series of defensive processes.
Q3. How does vaccination work according to the text? (06 pts)
A vaccine introduces a harmless form of a germ into the body, teaching the immune system to recognise it without causing the disease.

   An average person is estimated to contain trillions of human cells. These cells include neurons (nerve cells) and glands. The human body is covered by the skin, which is a flexible covering that protects the body and keeps water and harmful germs out. The body's strong internal framework — the skeleton — is made of 206 bones connected at joints, such as in the legs. These joints help us to move. The backbone supports the head and limbs and protects the spinal cord.

   Between the skeleton and the skin, there are about 500 muscles. Nerves spread from the brain to all parts of the body, carrying signals in the form of tiny electrical impulses. The sense organs — eyes, nose, ears, tongue and skin — pass messages to the brain through nerves.

   The brain controls every activity of the body. It receives information from the senses and sends instructions to the muscles. The brain also controls our thoughts, feelings, and memories. It is divided into three main parts: the cerebrum, the cerebellum, and the brain stem, each responsible for different functions.

   A healthy body requires proper nutrition, regular exercise, and sufficient rest. The food we eat provides the energy needed for all body functions. Vitamins and minerals support the immune system and help the body grow and repair itself.

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

"Write about how healthy food and exercise affect our physical and mental health."
Our physical and mental health are closely linked to the lifestyle choices we make every day. Two of the most important factors are healthy eating and regular exercise.

A balanced diet provides the body with the energy, vitamins, and minerals it needs to function properly. Foods rich in vitamins C and D strengthen the immune system, while protein-rich foods help repair tissues and build muscles. Avoiding excessive sugar, salt, and processed foods reduces the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and obesity.

Regular physical exercise also plays a vital role in maintaining good health. It strengthens the heart, improves blood circulation, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Moreover, exercise has proven mental health benefits: it reduces stress, anxiety, and depression by releasing endorphins — chemicals in the brain that promote feelings of happiness.

In conclusion, a healthy diet combined with regular exercise is the best investment we can make in our long-term well-being. Good health is truly our most valuable asset.
