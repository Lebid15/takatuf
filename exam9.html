I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   The Right to Education

   The right to education is one of the basic universal rights of the human being. It is one of the goals that the education schedule supports and is expected to be achieved by the year 2030 in order to achieve sustainable development aimed at by the United Nations. It is a force that enables individuals to get rid of poverty and achieve individual and social well-being. It is something mandatory for the state, guaranteed by its own law, for all individuals without any form of discrimination. The right to education includes a number of freedoms and rights that individuals are entitled to: the right to have primary education for all, free of charge; the right to have secondary education available to everyone without exception; and equal access to higher education without any distinction or discrimination.

   Education is the right for parents to choose freely appropriate schools for their children and enrol them in, regardless of their beliefs and religions. In addition, it is the right to freely establish any educational institution that adheres to the standards set by the government and the state regarding students and academic staff.

   Education is of great importance because of its great role in building society, which is made up of all segments of teachers, leaders, politicians and others. Through education, people get to know other cultures and worlds, and it helps to enhance understanding and communication between them. It also helps in bringing fun and adventure by getting to know new things, interests and new people. Through education, a person can build a social network and develop different life skills by applying what one learns in various aspects of economic, social and political life.

   Here in Syria, the government is working hard to promote all these principles in society through free education and adopting new methods of teaching.

   Simply speaking, human rights are the rights which one acquires by being alive, while civil rights are the rights that one obtains by being a legal member of a certain political life. Civil rights are the rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality. They guarantee equal social opportunities and equal protection under the law, regardless of race, religion, or national origin.

   Civil and political rights are a class of rights that protect individuals' freedom from violation by governments, social organisations, and private individuals. They ensure one's right to participate in the civil and political life of society and the state without discrimination or repression. Unlike human rights or natural rights, which people acquire inherently, civil rights must be given and guaranteed by the power of the state.

   Examples of civil rights include the right to vote, the right to a fair trial, the right to government services, the right to a public education, and the right to use public facilities. Civil rights are an essential component of democracy; when individuals are prevented from participating in political society, their civil rights are being violated.

   Throughout history, many groups have had to fight for their civil rights. The civil rights movement in the United States during the 1950s and 1960s, led by figures such as Martin Luther King Jr., is one of the most famous examples of a struggle for equal rights under the law.

In Syria, the government has made great efforts to promote education. Schools are provided free of charge for all children, and education is compulsory at the primary level. The government has also invested in building new schools, training teachers, and developing modern curricula that prepare students for the challenges of the 21st century.

In my opinion, education must remain a top priority for every government. The more we invest in education today, the better our society will be tomorrow. I believe that a country's true wealth lies not in its natural resources, but in the education and skills of its citizens.
