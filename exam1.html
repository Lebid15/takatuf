I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Moving to a country with a very different climate could be a challenge! In Syria we have a Mediterranean weather, with mild, rainy winters. Here in Canada winter is very cold and snowy. Temperature average is between the single digits and the -20s. I was very worried about the cold. But it wasn't a problem because most buildings here are well heated. Actually, this city knows how to stay warm. When the weather gets too chilly, I head to the Underground City – a series of interconnected tunnels beneath Montreal which run for over 32 kms. The tunnels connect shopping malls, universities, banks and seven metro stations. I miss my mom's cooking a lot, the food here is delicious but it's not really the same. Foods, typically considered national dishes of Canada, include poutine, Montreal bagels and butter tarts. My most amazing experience was Sugar time, usually at the end of March. It is the season when maple syrup is produced, and some parties are organised in "la cabane à sucre'', a place where maple sap is boiled to produce maple syrup.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. Is the winter in Syria similar to the winter in Canada? If not, why? (06 pts)
No, it is not similar. In Syria, the weather is Mediterranean with mild and rainy winters, while in Canada winter is very cold and snowy, with temperatures between the single digits and -20s.
Q2. What does she do when the weather is too cold? (06 pts)
When the weather gets too chilly, she heads to the Underground City – a series of interconnected tunnels beneath Montreal.
Q3. When does Sugar time take place? (06 pts)
Sugar time usually takes place at the end of March. It is the season when maple syrup is produced.
Find words in the text which mean the following: (12 marks)
Q4. Very cold. (06 pts)
chilly
Q5. Underground street which connects two areas. (06 pts)
tunnel
Rewrite these sentences about the text to correct the information: (12 marks)
Q6. The temperature in Syria is between the single digits and the -20s. (06 pts)
The temperature in Canada (not Syria) is between the single digits and -20s. In Syria, the weather is mild and rainy.
Q7. "la cabane à sucre" is the name of syrup. (06 pts)
"la cabane à sucre" is not the name of syrup. It is a place where maple sap is boiled to produce maple syrup.
II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   An X-ray is a common imaging test that has been used for decades. It is an image created on a photographic film or electronically on a digital system, using an external radiation to produce images of the body. X-ray Imaging can help doctors view the inside of the patient's body without having to make an incision. This can help surgeons diagnose, monitor and treat many medical conditions.

   Robotic Surgery is usually associated with minimally invasive procedures. During Robotic Surgery, surgeons can perform very complex steps with more precision, flexibility and control than it is possible with conventional techniques. Compared to traditional open surgery, Robotic Surgery results in smaller incisions which reduce pain and lead to less time spent in hospitals and quicker recovery time. The emerging medical devices and technologies, especially the mobile phones and tiny Nano-sized sensors embedded in several electronic devices that enable sending and receiving information wirelessly, are changing the face of 21st century medical practice.

III– Choose the correct answer a, b, c, or d: (54 marks)
Q14. Mrs. Duncan is talking to her students ……… projects are due on Friday. (06 pts)
a- whose    b- whom    c- where    d- who
a- whose (possessive relative pronoun — "the students' projects are due")
Q15. Invest if you like, but you're ……… the risk of losing everything if the business fails. (06 pts)
a- meeting    b- giving    c- running    d- losing
c- running (to run a risk = to take a risk)
Q16. The /ed/ in the verb ……… is pronounced /d/ (06 pts)
a- reminded    b- phoned    c- started    d- dressed
b- phoned /foʊnd/ — ends in voiced /n/ → /d/ sound
(reminded → /ɪd/; started → /ɪd/; dressed → /t/)
Q17. The vet dissected the animal and ……… its organs. (06 pts)
a- extracted    b- rotated    c- pressed    d- reacted
a- extracted (to extract = to remove/take out)
Q18. Seldom ……… newspapers nowadays. (06 pts)
a- do we read    b- we have read    c- did we read    d- we read
a- do we read (subject-auxiliary inversion after negative adverb "Seldom" at the start)
Q19. Either the teacher or the student ……… planned to come. (06 pts)
a- are    b- is    c- have    d- has
d- has (verb agrees with the nearest subject "the student" — singular)
Q20. You ……… harder for the exam last term. Your results were too bad. (06 pts)
a- should study    b- must study    c- should have studied    d- don't have to study
c- should have studied (criticism / regret about a past action)
Q21. To jump out of your skin means that you are ……… (06 pts)
a- excited    b- shocked    c- relaxed    d- stayed for a long time
b- shocked (idiom: "jump out of your skin" = to be very startled or frightened)
Q22. Someone tells you that their dog can sing pop songs. What do you reply to him? (06 pts)
a- I'm not surprised.    b- No wonder.    c- Do you seriously expect me to believe that?    d- That's absolutely amazing!
c- Do you seriously expect me to believe that? (expressing disbelief at an unbelievable claim)

Q25. Doctors need …… sample of your urine, blood or swab from your nose. (06 pts)

Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on ONE of the following topics:

1- Write about a personal experience in which you failed but then you could achieve your target.

2- Write a paragraph about the effects of the food on our brains and bodies.
Topic 1 – Model Answer:

Last year, I failed my first English exam. I had not studied well and felt ashamed of my result. However, instead of giving up, I decided to change my approach. I began to study more regularly, practise writing every day, and ask my teacher for help.

After several weeks of hard work, I felt more confident. When the next exam came, I gave my best, and I achieved an excellent mark. This experience taught me that failure is not the end; it is the beginning of a new effort. I now believe that with determination and patience, anyone can overcome obstacles and achieve their goals.
