I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   As a result of the substantial scientific and technological development that affected many disciplines, many branches have appeared within the specialisation of engineering in particular, including medical technology engineering, which is also called biomedical engineering. This field is concerned with studying the body from an engineering point of view, in order to design suitable artificial limbs, organs, or other medical devices.

   Medical devices can be divided into two parts, diagnostic medical devices, most notably the ultrasound device, and medical therapeutic devices used in chemical treatments. Another classification includes three categories. Firstly, we have simple devices such as bandages, body temperature meters and others. There are also more complex devices that require a certain amount of caution, such as those that emit X-rays. Finally, there are those which require a deep study before their use, such as the valves used in the artificial heart, in addition to the artificial joints.

   This will allow huge amounts of data storage, the thing which will be reflected in the robot's ability to fulfil a lot of operational tasks. Consequently, the use of robotics will be expanded.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. What is medical technology engineering concerned with? (06 pts)
Medical technology engineering (biomedical engineering) is concerned with studying the body from an engineering point of view, in order to design suitable artificial limbs, organs, or other medical devices.
Q2. What are the two parts of medical devices? (06 pts)
The two parts are: 1) diagnostic medical devices (e.g. the ultrasound device), and 2) medical therapeutic devices used in chemical treatments.
Q3. What kind of devices need more caution? (06 pts)
The more complex devices that require caution are those that emit X-rays. The most caution is needed for devices like the valves used in the artificial heart and the artificial joints.

Rewrite these sentences about the text to correct the information: (12 marks)
Q6. The ultrasound devices is an example of medical therapeutic devices. (06 pts)
The ultrasound device is an example of diagnostic medical devices, not therapeutic ones.
Q7. Doctors use the valves in the artificial joints. (06 pts)
Doctors use the valves in the artificial heart, not in the artificial joints. (The joints are mentioned separately.)
II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   The two most common causative agents of infectious diseases are the virus and the bacterium. They both are tiny to be seen by the naked eye, they can cause similar symptoms and are often spread in the same way. Bacteria are living organisms but viruses aren't. A bacterium is a single complex cell. It can survive on its own, inside or outside the body. This allows bacteria to live in many places – soil, water, plants and the human body. We have many bacteria on and inside our body, especially in the gut to help digest food. On the other hand, viruses are smaller and aren't cells. Unlike bacteria, they need a host such as a human or an animal to multiply. Their life involves the hijacking of the biochemical activities of a living cell. In fact, it is very important to know whether bacterial or viral causes cause an infection because the treatments differ. It can be very difficult to know what causes an infection because viral and bacterial infections can cause similar symptoms.
Choose the correct answer a, b, c or d: (24 marks)
Q8. Viruses and bacteria can't be seen by the naked eye because ……… (06 pts)
a- they are harmful    b- they are aggressive    c- they are small    d- they are huge
c- they are small
Q9. Bacteria are ……… viruses. (06 pts)
a- bigger than    b- smaller than    c- as they as    d- tinier than
a- bigger than (bacteria are single complex cells; viruses are smaller and aren't cells)
Q10. The word in the text that refers to a single living unit is ……… (06 pts)
a- virus    b- bacterium    c- cell    d- way
b- bacterium (a bacterium is described as a single complex cell)
Q11. The opposite of "similar" in the text is ……… (06 pts)
a- important    b- natural    c- different    d- familiar
c- different

Q13. It is difficult to know what infection you have because ………………………… (06 pts)

III– Choose the correct answer a, b, c, or d: (54 marks)
Q14. She hated to say the words for fear of causing ……… to him. (06 pts)
a- pain    b- painful    c- painless    d- painfully
a- pain (noun form needed after the verb "causing")
Q15. This is our fifth win. We are ……… (06 pts)
a- taking a turn for the worse    b- by the book    c- fared off    d- on a roll
d- on a roll (idiom = having a series of successes)
Q16. I have a terrible acne. I should see a ……… (06 pts)
a- cardiologist    b- dermatologist    c- pediatrician    d- allergist
b- dermatologist (specialist in skin conditions)
Q17. I slipped as I stepped ……… the platform. (06 pts)
a- onto    b- out of    c- into    d- for
a- onto (to step onto a surface)
Q18. One of the following has a final voiceless sound: (06 pts)
a- bed    b- judge    c- pet    d- sing
c- pet /pɛt/ ends in /t/ which is voiceless (bed→/d/, judge→/dʒ/, sing→/ŋ/ are all voiced)
Q19. The new clerk didn't ……… his boss's expectations. (06 pts)
a- make    b- meet    c- mean    d- ran
b- meet (to meet expectations = to fulfil / satisfy expectations)
Q20. You ……… wait here. It isn't allowed. (06 pts)
a- mustn't    b- should    c- have to    d- shouldn't
a- mustn't (prohibition — it is forbidden to wait here)
Q21. She was too young to ……… in the national elections. (06 pts)
a- elect    b- elections    c- elected    d- vote
d- vote (to vote in the elections — infinitive needed after "to")
Q22. The wheel began to ……… quickly. (06 pts)

d- rotate (to rotate = to turn / spin)

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

Many people visit your country, but they don't know a lot about it. Write an article to help them be culturally aware of your country.
Syria is an ancient country with a rich history and a vibrant culture. It is home to some of the oldest cities in the world, including Damascus and Aleppo. Visitors are often amazed by the beautiful mosques, old markets (souks), and historical ruins scattered across the land.

The Syrian people are known for their hospitality. If you visit, you will be welcomed with warm tea and delicious food. Syrian cuisine includes dishes such as kibbeh, falafel, and the famous Syrian sweets. The country also has a tradition of storytelling, music, and craftsmanship that dates back thousands of years.

To truly understand Syria, one must explore its culture, meet its people, and appreciate its heritage. Syria is not just a country; it is a living museum of human civilisation.
