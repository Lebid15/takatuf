I– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   The word "robot" stems from the Czech "robota" which means a "slave". Man has built a robot after his/her image. Possibly, that is why human beings have a variety of strong emotions, both negative and positive towards robots.

   The greatest advantage of robots and intelligent machines is accompanied with their ability to play a range of movements and thinking endlessly and tirelessly. When designing robots, it is essential for planners to focus on the robots' ability to follow patterns, due to this engineering reality; robots are constantly specialised in the first step. Another complicated sort is the generalized robot, which can to some extent imitate humans. In the future, the memory of robots is expected to become very extensive.

   This will allow huge amounts of data storage, the thing which will be reflected in the robot's ability to fulfil a lot of operational tasks. Consequently, the use of robotics will be expanded.
Answer the following questions: (18 marks)
Q1. Why do humans have different emotions towards robots? (06 pts)
Because man has built robots after his own image, human beings have a variety of strong emotions, both negative and positive, towards them.
Q2. What is the crucial thing for designers to pay close attention to? (06 pts)
It is essential for designers to focus on the robots' ability to follow patterns.
Q3. How is the generalized robot different? (06 pts)
The generalized robot can to some extent imitate humans, which makes it different from the specialised robot that only focuses on one step.
Find words in the text which mean the following: (12 marks)
Q4. A person owned by another person. (06 pts)

Q5. Type or kind. (06 pts)

Rewrite these sentences about the text to correct the information: (12 marks)
Q6. The word "robot" has a German origin. (06 pts)
The word "robot" does not have a German origin. It stems from the Czech word "robota".
Q7. Nowadays, the memory of robots is very big. (06 pts)
Nowadays, the memory of robots is not very big yet. It is expected to become very extensive in the future.
II– Read the following text then do the tasks below:
   Through innovation and e-government, governments around the world can be more efficient as they provide better services and respond to the demands of their citizens by transparency and liability. They can be more comprehensive and receive the trust of citizens in their governments. E-government services have three classifications. Firstly, Government-to-Government (G2G) involves sharing data and conducting electronic exchanges between governmental sectors, as well as exchanges between the national, regional, and local levels. Secondly, Government-to-Business (G2B) includes business-specific transactions (e.g. payments, sale, and purchase of goods and services) as well as defaulting types of business-focused services. Finally, Government-to-Consumer (G2C) comprises initiatives designed to simplify citizens' interaction with the government as consumers of public services and contribute to the consultation and decision-making process.

III– Choose the correct answer a, b, c, or d: (54 marks)
Q14. The new ……… of the book will be published next week. (06 pts)
a- author    b- edition    c- round    d- prefix
b- edition (a new edition of a book = a new published version)
Q15. Rarely ……… anyone using carriages nowadays. (06 pts)
a- you see    b- will you see    c- you will see    d- have you seen
b- will you see (inversion after negative adverb "Rarely" at start of sentence)
Q16. Only after ……… , he travelled to London to study. (06 pts)
a- he graduated    b- he had graduated    c- did he graduate    d- he has graduated
b- he had graduated (Past Perfect for action completed before another past action)
Q17. Have you any idea where ……… Sam? (06 pts)
a- can I meet    b- I can meet    c- do I meet    d- have I met
b- I can meet (embedded question — no inversion in indirect questions)
Q18. There ……… to hold a meeting. (06 pts)
a- goes he    b- he goes    c- went he    d- he went
b- he goes (inverted: "There goes he" with informal order — or simply "There he goes")
Q19. The wheel began to ……… very quickly. (06 pts)
a- rotate    b- utilise    c- insert    d- pull
a- rotate
Q20. The author ……… his novel out last year. (06 pts)
a- put    b- got    c- picked    d- took
a- put (to put out = to publish / release)
Q21. Hama, being an ancient city, ……… the past and present everywhere you walk. (06 pts)
a- combination    b- combinable    c- combinational    d- combines

Q22. The ……… of the sun's radiation can cause skin cancer. (06 pts)

X– Composition: (50 marks)
Q40. Write a composition of no less than 80 words on the following topic:

Write an essay about the arguments for or against e-learning at school.
E-learning has become an increasingly popular method of education, especially after the global pandemic. It offers many advantages, but it also has some drawbacks that must be considered.

On the one hand, e-learning gives students the flexibility to study at their own pace and from the comfort of their homes. It provides access to a wide range of resources, videos, and interactive tools that make learning more engaging. Students in remote areas can also benefit greatly from e-learning.

On the other hand, e-learning requires a reliable internet connection, which is not available to everyone. Students may also lack the discipline to study independently without a teacher present. Furthermore, the lack of face-to-face interaction can lead to social isolation.

In conclusion, e-learning is a valuable tool when used alongside traditional education, but it should not completely replace classroom learning.
